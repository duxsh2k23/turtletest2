ABOUT

The hatchery plays a vital role in the preservation of sea turtles by collecting and incubating their eggs. The process involves locating turtle nests along the coastline and carefully transferring the eggs to the hatchery's protected environment. This ensures that the eggs have a higher chance of survival compared to the vulnerable nests on the beach.


Once the eggs hatch, the hatchery releases the baby turtles into the ocean during evening hours. This practice allows the hatchlings to make their way into the water under natural conditions, increasing their chances of survival. Visitors are often invited to witness this awe-inspiring event, providing them with an unforgettable experience and a deeper understanding of the conservation efforts involved.


In addition to its primary focus on hatching and releasing sea turtles, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery also serves as a rehabilitation center for injured or sick turtles. The dedicated staff at the hatchery provides medical care and attention to turtles that have been affected by various factors, including fishing nets, pollution, or other human-related activities. After their recovery, these turtles are released back into the ocean, contributing to the preservation of their species.
